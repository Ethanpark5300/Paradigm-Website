Frequently Asked Questions

Which insurances does Paradigm Psychiatry accept?
Paradigm Psychiatry accepts Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS), Cigna, TriCare, and United HealthCare (Optum).
What are the fees for self-pay patients?
For self-pay patients, the fee for a new patient visit is $250, and the fee for a follow-up visit is $150.
Can I request medical documents/forms such as FMLA and STD forms?
Medical documents/forms, including FMLA and STD forms, will be considered only for patients established for at least two months, and a fee will be applied.
What is the recommended arrival time for new patients?
New patients are encouraged to arrive 15 minutes prior to their scheduled appointment time to allow enough time for the registration and check-in process.

What happens if I miss or late-cancel an appointment?

First late cancellation/missed appointment: Courtesy reschedule.


Second late cancellation/missed appointment: $50.00 fee will be applied to your account.


Third late cancellation/missed appointment: $50.00 fee will be applied to your account and may result in discharge from the practice.

What if I am running late to my appointment?
If you are more than 10 minutes late to your appointment, please contact the office by phone to notify them as soon as possible. Paradigm Psychiatry has a late arrival policy, and you may be asked to reschedule to minimize wait times and provide quality care to all patients.
Which insurance plans does Paradigm Psychiatry accept?
Paradigm Psychiatry accepts the following commercial insurance plans: Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS), United Health Care (UHC), United Behavioral Health (UBH), Aetna, Cigna, Tricare West
Does Paradigm Psychiatry accept Medicare or Medicaid/AHCCCS plans?
No, Paradigm Psychiatry is not contracted with and does not accept any Medicare or Medicaid/AHCCCS plans. Patients are welcome to schedule as self-pay.
Is Paradigm Psychiatry contracted with Arizona Care Network, Ambetter, or Gilsbar?
No, Paradigm Psychiatry is out of network and not contracted with Arizona Care Network, Ambetter, and Gilsbar. Patients with these plans may need to consider self-pay options.
