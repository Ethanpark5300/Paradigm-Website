Meet the Providers

Alexandra Moore

DNP, PMHNP-BC

Dr. Alexandra (Allie) Moore is a Family Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner. She graduated with her Doctor of Nursing Practice from Arizona State University and is board certified through the American Nurses Credentialing Center. Dr. Moore is compassionate and uses a holistic approach to patient care.

She began her training with a Bachelor of Science in Nursing through an accelerated program at St. Joseph’s Hospital campus of Grand Canyon University. Allie furthered her education with Grand Canyon University and obtained a Master of Science in Nursing with an emphasis in nursing education as well. Her nursing background includes pediatric intensive care, pre-operative, and post anesthesia, as well as inpatient psychiatry at Phoenix Children’s Hospital. Allie has a passion for teaching and is a professor at Creighton University.

Dr. Moore knows each patient has unique needs and will tailor care specific to each patient. She treats patients with dignity, respect, and empathy in a non-judgmental and kind manner. She works with patients to provide stability and meet their treatment goals.

In her spare time Dr. Moore enjoys travelling, running, reading, and spending time with her dogs, family, and friends.

Jill Abernathy

PMHNP-BC

Jill Abernathy is a Family Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner. She graduated from Walden University with her Masters of Advance Nursing Practice and is board certified through the American Nurses Credentialing Center. Jill works with patients across the lifespan and has extensive experience working with children and adolescents in the mental health field. While Jill’s mental health expertise allows her to treat symptoms during visits, her true passion lies in uncovering root issues. By addressing deeper causes of mental health issues, clients can more fully embrace their authentic selves.

Over the years, Jill has gained experience working in diverse settings, including: inpatient psychiatric hospitals, intensive outpatient substance abuse programs, assertive community treatment (ACT), and crisis intervention. Jill has dedicated her career to helping people see their potential by encouraging positive change in her patients. Through therapeutic practice that combines medication management with alternative modalities such as yoga, breath-work, and mindfulness, Jill believes clients have better outcomes. She focuses on treating patients holistically by creating an environment that reduces stigma and allows patients to tailor their goals through individualized treatment plans.

In addition to her professional experience, in her personal life, Jill enjoys learning and utilizing alternative therapies including practicing yoga regularly to stay grounded. She loves adventure travel, scuba diving, being in nature, and spending time with her family. She truly has a passion for helping others to become their best selves.

Kionglee Jung

PMHNP, FNP - BC

Kionglee (Lee) Jung is a Board Certified Psychiatric Mental Health and Family Nurse Practitioner. She graduated with her Doctor of Nursing Practice in Family Practice from Arizona State University in 2014 and completed her Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner Certificate. Her passion strongly lies in building strong patient-provider relationships while providing professional and empathetic care to patients and their families.

Through the years, she has gained her Bachelor of Science in Nursing from the University of Massachusetts - Amherst and a diploma in Clinical Research from Toronto, Canada. Most of her nursing career of more than 10 years were in hospitals and clinical research trials located in Massachusetts and Toronto. She has settled down with her family in beautiful Arizona over 10 years ago. She speaks English, Korean and Malay.

Her focus is on treating a variety of psychiatric disorders that range in severity, including depression, anxiety, PTSD, ADHD, mood disorders, bipolar, and psychosis. Her many years of training, education, and experience allow her to effectively evaluate, diagnosis and treat her patients. She utilizes a combination of supportive therapy and medication management to achieve mental health stability and wellness in her patients. She creates a nonjudgmental environment and takes time with everyone to ensure there is a strong trust and connection.
